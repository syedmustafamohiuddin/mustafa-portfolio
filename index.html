I grew up chasing precision — first on the tatami as a competitive judoka, where timing, leverage, and control were everything. When I transitioned from sport to engineering, I brought that same obsession with feedback, force, and reaction into the world of mechatronics.

What started as curiosity for how machines move turned into safety-certified force control at Siemens Healthineers, autonomous underwater robotics at Kraken, and a constant drive to build intelligent systems at the intersection of the physical and digital worlds.

I don't just build machines. I build systems that respond, adapt, and make decisions in real time — from the operating room to the ocean floor. Whether programming torque-limiting algorithms, integrating LIDAR sensors, or optimising marine battery systems, I'm drawn to problems where engineering decisions carry real stakes.
