01 — Bachelor Thesis
Project 01  /  2024–2025
Intelligent Force Limitation
for Medical CT Motion Systems

Designed and implemented a real-time torque-controlled force limitation algorithm for Siemens Healthineers' CT gantry systems — enabling safe human-machine interaction under ISO 15066 and IEC 60601-2-44. The work introduced a "Single Click Activation" paradigm that eliminated manual reconfiguration across motion variants.

Built custom electromechanical test rigs to simulate both dynamic load collisions and nominal motion scenarios. Authored full certification-ready compliance documentation for regulatory submission.

Challenge
Real-time torque control across heterogeneous CT hardware variants without hardware modification
Outcome
Safety-certified multi-model algorithm with unified single-click activation across all variants
Impact
Compliance documentation accepted for regulatory review; deployed to next-generation CT platform

Project 02  /  Siemens Healthineers, 2024
LiDAR-Guided Collision Avoidance for Nexaris Angio CT

Integrated SICK LiDAR sensors into the sliding gantry architecture of the Nexaris Angio CT to deliver real-time spatial awareness and collision prevention. Developed embedded C/C++ detection logic with safety-rated PLC integration, achieving reliable obstacle detection while eliminating false-positive triggers that previously required manual intervention. Full PLM lifecycle documentation maintained for regulatory traceability.

03 — Marine Robotics
Project 03  /  Kraken Robotics, 2023–2024
AUV Power Optimisation & Telemetry Analysis

At Kraken Robotics, I led MATLAB-driven battery discharge analysis for autonomous underwater vehicles operating in harsh marine environments. By modelling discharge curves against depth, temperature, and mission profiles, I identified critical inefficiencies in the power distribution architecture. Telemetry trend dashboards were developed for field teams, enabling mission-critical decision-making. Environmental stress testing and FMEA on power subsystems delivered an 8% improvement in operational uptime.

Key Result
8% AUV operational uptime improvement through systematic power modelling and FMEA
MATLAB FMEA Battery Systems Telemetry Subsea Robotics
04 — Simulation
Project 04  /  Siemens Healthineers, 2024
Digital Twin — Precision Motion Dynamics

Developed a high-fidelity MATLAB/Simulink model replicating load-dependent motor torque profiles across the full dynamic range of CT gantry motion. The digital twin was validated against empirical sensor data from instrumented test rigs, enabling early-stage algorithm validation before hardware integration. PID control loop tuning was performed entirely in simulation, reducing physical iteration cycles by over 60%.

Impact
60%+ reduction in physical test iterations through pre-validated simulation
MATLAB Simulink PID Control Digital Twin Systems Modelling
05 — HIL / CAD
Project 05  /  Siemens Healthineers, 2024
HIL-Based Cable Routing & EMC Simulation

Engineered dynamic cable stress simulation in Siemens NX for a multi-axis medical imaging gantry, covering the full motion envelope from design to virtual life-cycle validation. EMC shielding and insulation coordination were verified through hardware-in-the-loop methodology, enabling identification of abrasion failure modes before physical prototyping. Results fed directly into engineering change requests and design review documentation.

Siemens NX EMC HIL Motion Simulation DFM

Production Optimisation & Mechanical Design

Conducted WIP and throughput simulation to identify production bottlenecks, achieving a 10% reduction in cycle time. Also designed custom jigs, fixtures, and tooling to support assembly quality and repeatability.
